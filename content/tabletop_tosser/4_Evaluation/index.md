We had to do a small user study for which I asked friends and my family. They all got time to get used to the game and than they performed a whole run were I tracked how they are playing. I divided the study into two parts. The first part is based on the game mechanics: how easy was it to understand, how well did they perform on the two skill based minigames (reaction and T-shape) and fun. The second part is based on the VR-implementation: motion sickness, presence and fatigue. In the end the participants could give some feedback.

My mother tried to play the game, but since she normally have a problem with dizziness, she got so much motion sickness after the first jump, that she couldn't play more.

**Part 1**

As you can see in the first figure the game mechanics were easy to understand. The fun is more diverse. The game mechanics itself were good, but the motivation was too low, since there is no real reward. You can compare with others or your previous run, how fast you were or how many minigames you had to play, but the random factor is too big to really compare with.

{{< figure src="images/part_1_1.png" caption="Here are the results of part 1." >}}

Since the number how often the participants got the reaction game I took the average. You can see that in the T-shape interaction the average value is higher than the reaction game. But as I wished the result show, that they both are better than a random dice which has an expected value of 3.5.

{{< figure src="images/part_1_2.png" caption="Here are the results of the two skillbased minigames." >}}


**Part 2**

The motion sickness is very different between the players. It's mostly like the player said beforehand, if he get's easily motion sick or not. Fatigue is due high due to motion sickness or due to flickering of the world. For me: in one game I don't have fatigue but as a programmer you have to play it multiple times, and then I still don't have motion sickness but a lot of fatigue after two or three games. Presence is relative good, but it is not interesting enough to completely captivate the players.

{{< figure src="images/part_2.png" caption="Here are the results of part 2." >}}

**Feedback**

 - The idea is good, but with more skill based minigames it were better.
 - Place the objects on a height based of the height of the headset.
 - The world is flickering too much.

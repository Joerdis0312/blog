In the course "Interaction in Virtual and Augmented Reality" at TU Darmstadt, we were given the opportunity to work with the Meta Quest 2 and implement a project using Unity. To start developing with Unity in 3D, I followed the "Roll-a-Ball" tutorial from Unity itself. For our project, we got a little map with a parcours and had to implement our own locomotion and interaction technique.

{{< figure src="images/parcours_old.png" caption="This was the parcours we got." >}}

While exploring potential locomotion techniques, I initially considered something from animals, such as simulating bird flight through arm-swinging or controlling a four-legged creature with independent limb movements. However, seeking a more engaging concept and drawing inspiration from my interest in board games, I decided to transform the environment into a board game-style experience. After further thinking, I decided on a concept between a board game and Mario Party. I turned the parcours like the maps of Mario Party with fields and little minigames to get, in my case, the number of fields the player can jump forward. The minigames are independent, so you can expand the game with various minigames, where you can get a number of jumps. To include the interaction in my boardgame I designed it as a minigame. This idea was also a good entry for me to Unity since this concept has a lot of different, relatively small components which could be created individually.

Finally, the project concluded with a small user study and the creation of this blog to document the development process. As recommended, I utilized Hugo to build this documentation.

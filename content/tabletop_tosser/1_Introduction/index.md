In the course "Interaction in Virtual and Augmented Reality" at TU Darmstadt, we were given the opportunity to work with the Meta Quest 2 and implement a project using Unity. To start developing with Unity in 3D, I followed the "Roll-a-Ball" tutorial provided by Unity. For our project, we received a small map featuring a parcour and had to implement our own locomotion and interaction techniques.

{{< figure src="images/parcours_old.png" caption="This was the parcour we received." >}}

While exploring potential locomotion techniques, I initially considered animal-inspired movements, such as simulating bird flight through arm-swinging or controlling a four-legged creature with independent limb movements. However, seeking a more engaging concept and drawing inspiration from my interest in board games, I decided to transform the environment into a board game-style experience.

After further consideration, I settled on a concept bridging a classic board game and Mario Party. I converted the parcour into a map similar to those in Mario Party, featuring fields and small minigames. In my version, these minigames determine the number of fields a player can jump forward. The minigames are independent, allowing the game to be expanded with various challenges to earn jumps. To integrate interaction into my board game, I designed it as a specific minigame. This approach served as an excellent entry point to Unity, as the concept consists of several distinct, relatively small components that could be developed individually.

Finally, the project concluded with a small user study and the creation of this blog to document the development process. As recommended, I utilized Hugo to build this documentation.

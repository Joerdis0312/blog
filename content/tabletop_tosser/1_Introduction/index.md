In the course "Interaction in Virtual and Augmented Reality" at TU Darmstadt, we got the opportunity to use a Meta Quest 2 and implement a little project in Unity. To start developing with Unity in 3D, I followed the roll-a-ball tutorial from Unity itself. For our project, we got a little map with a parcours and had to implement our own locomotion and interaction technique.

{{< figure src="images/parcours_old.png" caption="This was the parcours we got." >}}

I had different ideas for the locomotion technique. I first thought of something from animals, like flying by swinging your arms like a bird, or if it is possible to move an animal with four legs, so that you have to move all four legs independently. But then I thought this is a little boring and since I really like playing board games, I came to the idea of turning the parcours into a board game. After further thinking, I came to a concept between a board game and Mario Party. I turned the parcours like the maps of Mario Party with fields and little minigames to get, in my case, the number of fields the player can jump forward. The minigames are independent, so you can expand the game with a lot of different little games, where you can get a number of jumps. To include the interaction in my boardgame I designed it as a minigame. This idea was also a good entry for me to Unity since this concept has a lot of different, relatively small components which could be created individually.

In the end, we had to do a small user study and write this blog as a documentation of our experiences. As recommended, I used Hugo to write this blog.
